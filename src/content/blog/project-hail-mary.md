__Spoilers ahead! You have been warned.__

About an hour ago, I watched Project Hail Mary with my family and wow, that was an incredibly beautiful movie.

I am in no way a movie buff, but I just wanted to write down some of my thoughts immediately after watching.

Project Hail Mary made me feel the same exact way as watching Interstellar for the first time. The sheer weight and uncertainty of being light years away from home, shouldering a humanity-saving space mission, allowed both of these movies to keep me hooked from start to end.

In a short synopsis, Ryan Gosling's character, Ryland Grace, is a science teacher who finds himself awake in a spaceship with all his crewmates deceased and no recollection of the past (reminded me a lot of the 2016 film, [Passengers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passengers_(2016_film))). Grace had been sent on a mission to save earth from becoming uninhabitably cold due to a sun-eating lifeform called "Astrophage." Along the way, he comes across a quadruped alien creature that has set out on a mission to save its own planet from the same fate. Much of Project Hail Mary is spent with Grace and his alien companion -- which he names "Rocky" -- as they learn how to communicate with one another and plan how to save their homes. Throughout the movie, viewers catch glimpses into the events leading up to this mission as Grace remembers more about his pre-astronaut self.

Don't be fooled by this intense description of the film, though, because it is filled with so many hilarious moments from Grace's sarcastic humor that he uses to cope with stress and his conflicting morals. This duality of Project Hail Mary is something that works super well and is done very tastefully. There's a wonderful balance of high-stakes, gut-wrenching, and comedic scenes that give a lot of satisfaction.

Another thing: I think the scene where Grace envelopes himself inside the Petrova Line is one of the most beautiful movie moments I've ever seen. So many of the space shots are awing, vivid, and absolutely gorgeous.

I also think that the plot is very interesting and done well; Project Hail Mary has a lot of story to get through and even though it exceeds 2hr 30min in runtime, it feels very condensed. Condensed isn't bad, though! The movie never felt slow and every scene felt useful towards the plot. But, I _do_ wish there were a greater focus on specific moments that made me think, _"hmm, I wonder how this came to be?"_.

The most glaring example of this to me is how Grace magically acquired computer software to translate Rocky's alien language to real-time, audible human speech. I mean seriously, this was a stretch. Grace went from learning Rocky's word for the number '1' in one scene to talking with Rocky conversationally using real-time translation in the next. It's not even just this, though. Grace is a leading scientist in his field. It's maybe believable that he has the brains to come up with a translation software and makes this amount of vocabulary progression in a year's time. What's not believable is that the grammars of Rocky's language and English has a 1:1 mapping. Every time that Rocky comes across an English word that it's unfamiliar with, there's always a single English word that it translates to. Every time that Rocky speaks, each one of its noises maps to a single English word and it just so happens that the amount of time it takes Rocky to make a sentence in its language is the same time it takes for the English translation of that sentence. A little unrelated, but also, where was the computer and speaker set up for Rocky's sentences to always be translated in studio-quality so matter where it was on the ship. I know it's a bit nitpicky, but I just felt myself really paying attention to it.

Some other notable plot holes that I noticed are:

* the lack of explanation on how they extracted the Taumoeba from Adrian.
* how Rocky and its ship were totally fine when Grace went on a 53-day detour to save Rocky when the Taumoeba evolved to escape the Xenonite.
* how the Taumoeba did not evolve to escape the pods used to deliver the samples on the over 10-year trip back to earth.
* how Grace is fed in the biodome.

Regardless of my criticisms, I thought Project Hail Mary achieved what it set out to do and was a phenominal movie. I think it probably has a good chance to already be the winner for Best Picture this year.

I also told a lot of my criticisms to my mom who read the book, and she said that the book addresses a lot of these things. Maybe that means I'll have to read the book and see for myself!

Overall, I would definitely place this movie in my top 10 and I would give it a 👎👎👎.
